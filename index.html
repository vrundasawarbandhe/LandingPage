VARIETIES

Navel

The orange most popular for commercial production or home gardening because the fruits are so easy to peel and the slices separate easily. Navel oranges are also seedless, and the sweet flavor is outstanding.

Cara Cara

Cara Cara oranges are round to slightly oval, from three to four inches in diameter, with medium to thick rind that is bright orange in color and features a slightly pebbly texture.

Blood oranges

Blood oranges are sweeter than other oranges. Their juice is delicious, but because it is quite a bit sweeter than classic orange juice, it ferments quickly and should be used or drunk the same day it's juiced.

Jaffa

A Jaffa orange, also called a Shamouti orange, is a citrus fruit that is grown in Jaffa, Israel.

Valencia

The Valencia orange is named after the city of Valencia in Spain, although its actual origins are unknown.

Seville

Seville oranges are best suited for juicing and zesting as their bitter and sour flesh is unpalatable when used raw. The juice and rind are used for both sweet and savory applications.
